About Me:

My name is Janet, and I was meant to travel.

I'd known it for a long time, but I'd never actually been able to experience the beauty of traveling, of immersing oneself fully in something and somewhere so unique and special and entirely captivating.
Little did I know, an extreme bout of depression was just the thing to help awaken my passion for exploration.
Towards the end of my sophomore year of college I decided a change was in order, and it was then that I began researching and applying to various Study Abroad programs.
I'll spare you the details, but fast-forward a few short months and I found out I was accepted to the yearlong program at the prestigious Trinity College Dublin.

Trinity College Dublin

I had never been out of the country, so needless to say I was incredibly excited to start my journey.

And I had decided on Ireland for a few specific reasons (in no particular order):

  • I have ancestors from Ireland (I know, I know, we all do...)
    • (Turns out, I'm around 50% Irish!)
  • I love the rain
  • Who doesn't love an accent?

And it's in Dublin where my adventures truly began.

My very first night in town, I went to buy a SIM-card for my phone and somehow got lost, wandering around and around the park that I soon realized was only a five minute walk from my apartment.
Soon enough, I joined some university organizations, found myself a job, and got started with my studies. I made sure that I was always ahead on my assigned readings, because I wanted to be able to use long weekends and bank holidays for local European travel. With the first opportunity I had, I went on a trip spanning three-countries, all visited in under one week. This pattern repeated every time I had a momentary break from school, and while some trips are more memorable than others, all of them helped to fuel my wanderlust. It was when I returned home from my time abroad that I made The Promise.
