22nd

Brooklyn, NY

I celebrated my 22nd birthday the same summer I moved to NYC for the first time. I had finally settled into an apartment with other people in my teaching program, and we had been out and exploring Brooklyn when I came across the bar that I'd eventually throw my birthday party at! Turns out, the bar is also in the neighborhood I ended up moving to, and have lived in for the better part of five years now. To me, the best part of this birthday was the ability to merge my worlds together - I had friends from home, friends from college, and new friends from my teaching program all come together to celebrate as we closed out our first summer before real-life began.

'Franklin Park', Crown Heights, Brooklyn, New York

23rd

Ireland, Scotland, England, & Denmark

Another promise I made myself was that I'd return to Ireland every two years to see my friends and explore new regions of the country. This trip was the first time I went back to Dublin since leaving from study abroad. I spent a few nights in Dublin before going off to explore other parts of the country, but came back into town to see Riverdance on the eve of my 22nd birthday. The next day, I boarded a ferry for Scotland. After spending a few days in and around Edinborough, I made my way to London. My final stop on this trip was in Copenhagen, Denmark. I saw and did a lot on this trip, so it's practically impossible to pick a favorite place/meal/memory.

25th

Spain & Portugal

Let me start by saying I do not do well in warm weather. And Spain & Portugal during the months of July and August are more than just warm...This trip took me all over the country of Spain (my friends and I basically drove around in a counterclockwise spiral, starting from Madrid and then up to Bilbao before beginning the spiral). Some of my work friends (and their friends) joined for various parts of this trip, so every place we visited felt different based on who we were visiting with. From NW Spain, we went down through Portugal, before heading back into Spain via Seville. The year we took this trip was the year of the terrorist attack in Barcelona, so we were all a little on-edge, but it was a great experience overall.

26th

West Coast Road Trip
Whistler, Vancouver, Seattle, Portland, Eugene, & San Francisco

To date, this is still one of my favorite trips. While I feel very fortunate to have traveled throughout much of Europe, I felt as though I hadn't experienced enough of my own continent. So for birthday 26, I decided I was going to rent a car and drive down Highway 1. I definitely took the scenic route for this trip! I started up in Canada and explored Whistler (better known for winter visits) and Vancouver before taking the Amtrak Cascades train down into Seattle. From there, I rented a car and drove around the surrounding areas. Unexpectedly, my favorite part of this trip was Portland. The people were all super friendly and super weird and it was just my kind of vibe. The coastal drive is something I truly will never forget.

Vancouver, British Columbia, Canada

Seattle, Washington

27th

Beaver Lake, NJ

This was an emotional birthday/trip/place. When my mom and her siblings were younger, her parents owned a house on this lake (which they eventually sold). When my cousins and I were kids, my grandparents would rent a house on the lake to have the family come and visit (all the cousins - the house was always packed). My grandfather died when I was 10 years old, so I hadn't been back to the lake in 17 years. As a place, Beaver Lake holds so many fond memories for me, and I thought it would be nice to stay around my family for once (as they never come on my birthday trips). So my mom and two of her siblings divvied up a two-month rental of a house on this lake, and we took turns living there throughout the summer. Unfortunately, my mom hurt her back pretty badly while staying at the lake, so she was unable to sleep there for most of the vacation. My parents would drive home every night, sleep there, and then drive the 40 minutes back to the lake the next day. Even though I intended to spend much of this summer with my parents, I actually ended up spending most of it alone. Still, Beaver Lake is one of my favorite places, but mostly for the memories it holds. I'm not sure I'll be strong enough to visit again.
